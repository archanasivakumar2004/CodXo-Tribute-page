Helen Keller

An Inspiration to Millions

Biography

Helen Adams Keller (June 27, 1880 - June 1, 1968) was an American author, political activist, and lecturer. She was the first deafblind person to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree.

Famous For

  • Overcoming the adversity of being deaf and blind
  • Becoming a prolific author, activist, and lecturer
  • Advocating for people with disabilities
  • Promoting women's suffrage and workers' rights

Her Sufferings

At 19 months old, Keller contracted an illness that left her deaf and blind. She lived in a world of darkness and silence until the age of 7 when Anne Sullivan became her teacher.

Education

Anne Sullivan played a crucial role in Helen's education. She taught Helen to communicate using the manual alphabet and Braille. Helen later attended Radcliffe College, graduating cum laude in 1904.

Her Books

  • The Story of My Life (1903)
  • Optimism (1903)
  • The World I Live In (1908)
  • Out of the Dark (1913)
  • My Religion (1927)

Her Friends

Helen had many famous friends, including Mark Twain, Alexander Graham Bell, Charlie Chaplin, and Eleanor Roosevelt.

Her Work

Keller devoted much of her life to social and political causes. She was a pacifist, a suffragist, and an advocate for people with disabilities. She co-founded the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) in 1920.

Life and Legacy

Helen Keller's life story has become a symbol of courage, perseverance, and hope for millions of people around the world. Her legacy continues to inspire people with and without disabilities.

Accomplishments

  • First deafblind person to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree
  • Published 12 books and numerous articles
  • Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1964
  • Traveled to over 40 countries as a goodwill ambassador

Why She is Celebrated

Helen Keller is celebrated for her indomitable spirit, her advocacy for marginalized groups, and her demonstration that disability is not inability. She showed the world that with determination and support, one can overcome seemingly insurmountable obstacles.
